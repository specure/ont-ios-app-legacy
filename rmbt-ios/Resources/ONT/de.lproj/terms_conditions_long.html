GENERAL TERMS AND PRIVACY POLICY FOR THE USE OF THE Example TEST NET SYSTEM FOR MEASURING THE QUALITY OF BROADBAND SERVICE

1 General Terms for the use of the Example Test Net system for measuring the quality of broadband service

The Agency for Communication Networks and Services (Agencija za komunikacijska omrežja in storitve Republike Slovenije; Example) offers users the opportunity to use the Example Test Net software to test the quality and capacity of their current broadband connection. Children under 15 can use the Example Test Net with parental consent or consent of their legal guardian.

Using Example Test Net on mobile devices may cause consumption of large data amount. By using it on 3G technology the usual data amount would be between 5 and 10 MB. On 4G data amount may exceed 100 MB. Both assumptions are done using quality service. Agency takes no responsibility for large data consumption done by user and therefore related costs.

Example Test Net provides users with transparent and detailed information on the current quality of their broadband access service while respecting their privacy. The test is based on the open source and open data principle. Open source principle means that the source code of Example Test Net software is published completely. This is mainly for transparency and reliability issues. Any commercial use of Example Test Net source code, which is published under the MAME license, is not allowed. The Example Test Net logo and font are not subject to the open source and open data principle and copying, modifying or processing them in changed or unchanged form is strictly prohibited. Example Test Net measurement results data are retrievable as Open Data and available for re-use under the Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 (CC BY 4.0), so they are freely available to the general public for information, use, dissemination and other applications. Data which are not retrievable as Open Data are not subject to the open data principle.

Links and references to other websites or persons outside the Agency are provided to facilitate use and should not be deemed as approved by the Agency. The Agency offers the service only to provide a greater and easier public access to information. The Agency respects the right to privacy and shall not collect any personal data without the explicit consent of users. The personal data which the Agency shall acquire on the basis of an explicit and informed consent of users shall be processed by complying with the highest security and confidentiality standards; i.e. pursuant to the Personal Data Protection Act (Zakon o varstvu osebnih podatkov; ZVOP-1).

Prior to using Example Test Net users must acknowledge their explicit acceptance of the Terms of Use and Privacy Policy of the Example Test Net system for measuring the quality of broadband service. By accepting the general Terms of Use and Privacy Policy users explicitly accept that the Agency (as the personal data administrator) collects and processes their personal data specified under section 2.1.1 for the purposes specified under section 2.1.2. The acceptance (YES/NO) is logged for evidential purposes that Terms of Use and Privacy Policy were accepted When changes are made to the processing and transmission of data (in connection with updates or new releases of the Example Test Net), users will have to renew their acceptance of the General Terms of Use and Privacy Policy.

You can send all questions regarding the personal data protection and privacy policy when using Example Test Net system for measuring the quality of broadband service to the Agency's email address: example@example.org.

The Example Test Net comprises two separate test sets that can be used completely independently:

  • Example Test Net is a general test, which measures ping (latency), download, upload and further quality parameters of the network connection

  • NDT Test (Network Diagnostic Tool test) is an additional more comprehensive test of the M-Lab research platform, which includes other technical parameters of the connection and can be performed only on the Android platform;

Users always have the option to run the Example Test Net only or the NDT Test as well, when they select the relevant option under Settings.

2.1 Example Test Net

2.1.1 What data are processed and transmitted?

During the Example Test Net for mobile devices with Android the following data are processed:

  • ping (latency), speed and quantity of transmitted data in both directions (downlink/uplink); *

  • Quality and transparency parameters (e.g. signal strength, connectivity on different ports, modifications of content, transfer time for a reference web page), and reliability of the measurement; *

  • Test parameters (test method, test progress, test server, test configuration (e.g. test duration));*

  • Randomly generated client ID (client UUID) and the number of performed tests;

  • Public client ID (changed every 24h);*

  • Client sync code and information on the client (language, local IP, version, test time and time band);*

  • Indicator of the network address translation mechanisms (NAT/PAT);*

  • IP version used (IPv4 or IPv6);*

  • Public IP address of the user;

  • WLAN network ID (WiFi SSID), numerical WLAN ID (WiFi BSSID) and encryption type;

  • Mobile network ID (home network, roaming network);*

  • Mobile technology used (EDGE, UMTS, HSPA, LTE) ;*

  • Name and country of mobile network and roaming status;*

  • Geographic location of the client and direction of movement;**

  • Type of device/model, OS/Example Test Net app version;**

  • Public measurement ID (generated upon each measurement);**

  • Anonymised public IP address (hidden last octet).

Data not marked with (*) are not transmitted (public) and shall not be disclosed. This data shall be accessed by the Agency only.

The data marked with asterisk (*) are transmitted; i.e. published and visible to everyone through the Example Test Net, and made freely available to the general public as Open Data for information, use and dissemination.

The data marked with two asterisks (**) are published and visible to everyone on the Example Test Net website and can be freely accessed as open data for information, use, dissemination and other application only upon explicit approval of the user. Users can at any time withdraw their acceptance of publication of the above specified data by unchecking the "Publication of personal data" checkbox in the "Settings" menu. Withdrawal of acceptance acts for future activities and it does not mean deleting old data stored In the event that before the first completed measurement the user does not approve the publication of data on the client's geolocation and direction of movement, type or model of device, OS and version of the Example Test Net app used, public measurement ID generated upon each measurement, and anonymised public IP address, the above-specified data shall not be published on the website. However, they shall remain visible in the history of the completed tests, which can be accessed by the user, and recorded in the Example Test Net database managed by the Agency. The only exception is the geographic location of the user, which is (in the event of absence of the user's approval) anonymised to the circle of 300m and published in this form.

2.1.2 The purpose of personal data collection

2.1.2.1 Primary database

All data acquired by the Agency acquired through the use of Example Test Net and results of all measurements (all data listed under section 2.1.1), which are carried out by users or the Agency are recorded in the primary database. The primary database can only be accessed by the employees of the Agency responsible for the management of the database. Users and the Agency shall use the Example Test Net system to measure the capacity and quality of network services provided by operators or service providers. The Agency shall use the collected data in order to monitor the situation and to carry out regulatory supervision in the field of fixed or mobile broadband services. The system shall show the Agency where in Slovenia and with which ISPs there are potential problems with access to internet services, where there is no mobile or wireless signal and whether operators are expanding the coverage of the specified white spots with mobile and wireless access pursuant to decisions on the assignment of radio frequencies.

All data listed under section 2.1.1 are kept in the primary database for a period of 10 years, with the exception of the user's public IP address, which is kept for a period of 6 months.

The user's public IP address is being processed in order to determine the network operator, to identify potential improper use of the system and in order to prepare a history of measurements for a specific user. After the completed test the only the network address of the IP address (anonymised public IP address) is recorded in the database and can be accessed in accordance with the open data principle. The full IP address is recorded in the primary database, but shall not be disclosed to public and can be viewed by the user only in the mobile app under "Details" or in the browser under the "History" tab.

Data on WLAN network ID is stored for the purposes of interpreting his own results of users by having overlook on this information to all his measurements stored. This is critical especialy when different WiFi access points are used.

2.1.2.2 Public database

The selected and anonymised data from completed measurements (marked with one asterisk (*) or two asterisks (**), when the user explicitly allows it) are published online and can be accessed through the www.example.org website, while allowing the interested public to gain an independent and realistic insight in the state of mobile, wireless and fixed communication systems and internet services on the territory of Slovenia. They provide users with information on the network coverage for a specific network or service provider at the level of regions, cities, districts or the most common regular communication routes. Information on mobile coverage is especially important for users, who travel on Slovenian territory on a daily basis, or users without the possibilities to access fixed broadband networks.

The data stored in the public database are kept for a period of 10 years.

2.2 NDT Test

The NDT Test (Network Diagnostic Tool Test) offers a more advanced speed measurement and network diagnostics, and is suitable for advanced network users. Besides measuring speed the NDT test also tries to find the causes for limited speed and provides a detailed report of the examined state.

2.2.1 What data are processed and transmitted?

The following data are processed and transmitted as part of the advanced NDT Test:

  • speed of data connection in both directions (downlink/uplink);

  • TCP/IP protocol parameters (e.g. TCP Receive window);

  • proportion of IP packet losses, connection errors (e.g. half duplex);

  • latency/jitter of data connection (ping);

  • test parameters (test method, test server, test configuration);

  • operating system (name/version) and processor architecture;

  • Java runtime environment (name/version);

  • IP version;

  • IP address (local/public);

  • host name of computer;

  • NAT and firewall status and data connection parameters;

  • test time;

  • transmitted test data.

The NDT Test is provided by M-Lab, which also processes and publishes data acquired with the test.

M-Lab is a consortium of partners (New America Foundation's Open Technology Institute, PlanetLab Consortium, Google Inc. and academic researchers from the research, industry and public sector and constitutes an ecosystem for independent monitoring of the operation of the global Internet. The operation of M-Lab depends primarily on the support and participation of likeminded people and organisations. Within M-Lab researchers can publish their open-source tools for monitoring internet services. The data acquired with these tools are published. The mission of M-Lab is to carry out advanced research of the internet and provide of useful information on broadband access to the general and interested public.

You can find more information on the NDT Test methodology on the M-Lab website. Data processed during the NDT Test are transmitted to the M-Lab research platform (www.measurementlab.net). This means that these data are published by M-Lab and made freely accessible to the general public for information, use, dissemination and other applications under M-Lab Privacy Policy. The measurement data are accessible online through the M-Lab platform website: https://code.google.com/p/m-lab/wiki/HowToAccessMLabData. Example Test Net Android app processes and transmits results of the NDT Test, which are visible for each specific test in the mobile app under the 'Details' section. These results are also visible in the 'History' tab of the browser version if the user synchronised measurement results from several devices.

2.2.2 Information relating to personal data and data traffic with non-EU countries

During the NDT-Test the IP address, which is allocated to the mobile terminal, is processed and transmitted to the M-Lab research platform outside the EU. This means that these data are permanently stored and published by M-Lab and are made freely accessible to the general public for information, use, dissemination and other applications under the M-Lab-Open Data policy. The data protection requirements in non-EU countries may be less strict than in the EU. Because the IP address is processed and transmitted outside the EU, this may mean that the identity of the user of the Example Test Net can be identified or is potentially identifiable, which could result in being able to establish the user’s personal identity within the meaning of Article 6 of the Personal Data Protection Act (ZVOP-1).

Test results acquired by Example Test Net are solely of informative and illustrative purposes as there are too many technical parameters undefined on the user side. Therefore test results doesn't represent any legal proof of broadband connection speed which could lead to any consumer complaints cases represented in front of Agency.

Prior to the first running of the NDT Test users must reaccept the General Terms of Use and Privacy Policy of the Example Test Net, and thus explicitly accept that their data are processed and transmitted outside the EU, after which they need to check the "I wish to run the optional NDT test" checkbox. The user thereby explicitly accepts that during the NDT Test the IP address and other collected data (section 2.2.1) are processed and transmitted outside the EU. Users may withdraw their acceptance of the NDT Test at any time (in the app under Settings, by deactivating the NDT test option). Thereafter, only Example Test Net will be performed.
